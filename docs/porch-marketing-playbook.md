# The Porch Kitchen — Local Marketing Playbook

A sequenced, do-this-in-order playbook for building local visibility for The Porch
Kitchen (Sebastopol, Sonoma County). Work top to bottom: the foundation phases make
every later campaign more effective. Check items off as you go.

> Companion tool: the **Facebook Group Manager** at
> `porchkitchen.stierbusinessgroup.com/social` holds the 35 local Facebook groups,
> their posting rules, and join links. The trackers for press contacts, partnerships,

## Phase 0 — Foundation: own your listings (do this FIRST)

Review-and-listing platforms feed directly into search visibility. Nothing else
performs well until these are claimed, accurate, and active. Free.

- [ ] **Google Business Profile** — claim/verify. Controls how you appear in Google
      Maps and Search; the single highest-leverage listing. Add hours, menu link,
      photos, ordering/reservation links.
- [ ] **Yelp** — claim the business page; add photos, hours, menu, attributes.
- [ ] **TripAdvisor** — claim the listing (matters in wine country given tourist traffic).
- [ ] **Keep all three current** — photos, hours, and menu accurate everywhere.
- [ ] **Respond to reviews** on all three, consistently. Review activity itself boosts
      search ranking; a steady response cadence signals an active, cared-for business.

## Phase 1 — Local presence: claim channels & start the habits

- [ ] **Nextdoor Business Page** — claim free at business.nextdoor.com. Reaches
      *verified residents* in surrounding neighborhoods (repeat local diners, not
      tourists). Post takeout/menu links; explore gift cards.
  - [ ] Set up **Local Deals** to push specials/discounts targeted down to specific
        neighborhoods.
  - [ ] Encourage neighbor **recommendations** — they function like word-of-mouth and
        resurface the business in feeds.
- [ ] **Eventbrite** — create an organizer profile. List tastings, special events, and
      opening-night happenings; these get picked up by event calendars and the local
      publications below.
- [ ] **Instagram habits** (see Reference for accounts + hashtags):
  - [ ] Follow and start engaging with @biteclubeats, @sonomacountyfoodblog, @sonomamag.
  - [ ] Tag location **"Sebastopol, Ca"** and **"The Porch"** in *every* post — measurably
        improves local discovery.
  - [ ] Use the canonical hashtag block (Reference) so local aggregators can find/repost.
- [ ] **Join the local business orgs** (your two best cross-promo partners):
  - [ ] Sebastopol Chamber of Commerce
  - [ ] Sebastopol Downtown Association

## Phase 2 — Outreach: earn local press (time-sensitive)

A new opening has a narrow press window and awards seasons are calendar-bound. A single
feature gets shared across all of an outlet's channels, so this is high-leverage right now.

- [ ] **Email Heather Irwin** (@biteclubeats) — Dining Editor for *both* Sonoma Magazine
      and the Press Democrat, the most influential food-media gatekeeper in the county.
      Her email is publicly listed at the bottom of her articles. A short, friendly intro
      pitch is completely standard and welcome. **(Use the template below.)**
- [ ] **Press Democrat — "New and upcoming Sonoma County restaurants"** roundups. They
      actively cover new openings — exactly our situation. Get on their radar.
- [ ] **Sonoma County Tourism** (sonomacounty.com, the official DMO) — get listed and
      pitch for restaurant guides / "top picks from local chefs."
- [ ] **Sonoma.com** — restaurant guide listing.
- [ ] **Sebastopol Times** — pitch a new-opening mention (active local news outlet).
- [ ] **Sonoma Magazine** lists — angle for "Best New Restaurants of the Year" and
      "Most Instagrammable Restaurants."
- [ ] **North Bay Bohemian** (bohemian.com) and **NorthBay biz** (northbaybiz.com) —
      pitch the alt-weekly and the business monthly.
- [ ] **Best of Sonoma County Readers' Choice Awards** (pressdemocrat.com/best) — get
      nominated and rally customers to vote when the window opens. Proven visibility play.

## Phase 3 — Ongoing & in-store

- [ ] **Table card / QR code** that makes it easy for happy customers to tag you on
      social and leave a review (Google/Yelp). The single cheapest review-velocity lever.
- [ ] **Reddit** — be a helpful, transparent presence in r/sonoma and r/santarosa. You
      *cannot* spam recommendation threads; the win is being organically recommended.
- [ ] **Restaurant Week** — participate when the countywide window opens (built-in reach).

## Smart networking strategies

Moves beyond just posting — relationships compound:

- **Wineries & tasting rooms** — partner for cross-promotion; wine-country diners move
  between dining and tasting constantly. Your highest-fit partnership category.
- **Breweries, hotels, local Airbnbs** — cross-refer guests looking for where to eat.
- **Food bloggers / influencers** — invite the local accounts (Reference) for a tasting
  visit; one good post from @sonomacountyfoodblog or a Heather Irwin mention outperforms
  dozens of self-posts.
- **Countywide programs** — Restaurant Week and the Best of Sonoma County awards come
  with built-in promotional reach; layer them in when their windows open.
- **Make reviews/tags frictionless** — the table card/QR above; ask at the right moment.
